Kombu Dashi

Photo by Shane Stagner on Unsplash.

Description

Dashi (出汁, だし) is a family of stocks used in Japanese cuisine. Dashi forms the base for miso soup, clear broth soup, noodle broth soup, and many simmering liquids to accentuate the savory flavor known as umami. Dashi is also mixed into the flour base of some grilled foods like okonomiyaki and takoyaki.

Kombu dashi is made by soaking or gently simmering kelp in water; soaking is traditional & fit for making baby food while simmering is a more modern practice. Kombu dashi becomes bitter & unpalatable when boiled.

Ingredients

  • 1 piece kombu (dried kelp)
  • 4 cups water

Steps

  1. Place the water in a pot and soak the kombu at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Place the pot on low heat and remove it from the heat just before it comes to a boil.
  3. Remove and dispose of the kombu. The resulting dashi stock can be stored in the refrigerator or used immediately.
